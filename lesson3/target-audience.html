Our Target Audience

We aim to serve all citizens within the town of Manassa but with the demographics within the town our majority would be Males aging 50 to 60 years of age and women in their early 60s. People that have lived here most of their lives or have a very strong connection to the area due to family connection or heritage. There are also a large number of teenagers living in the town that we would like to focus on to encourage growth and development through the coming years. These are people with a high school diploma and possibly some college. The average income is under 70k a year and a pointed focus on family relations and connection.

Personas

Jedediah Suthaby, DVM

Head Veterinary Doctor of Easy Care Vet Clinic/Owner

Doctor Suthaby is a strong supporter of the community and very familiar with the local population through his work and volunteer ventures. He is a trustworthy and outspoken 52-year-old father of 4. His work is highly demanding as he focuses on large animals and travels to accomodate local farmers and ranchers with their variety of needs. He runs and owns the local Vet clinic. As a long-time resident who spend several years outside the community obtaining his degree he has plenty to offer in growth without changing the desires of the local population to keep tight knit community strong. Famous for saying "Don't look a gift horse in the mouth."

Magdalena Rodriguez

Retired School Teacher

Having taught elementary school at the local Manassa Elementary for 30+ years, Ms. Rodriquez is a highly valued member of the community. Most of the new parents in the area were taught by Ms. Rodriquez and she is regarded as a pilar of the community. Having lived and taught her entire life in the same county as Manassa, Ms. Rodriquez has a vast understanding of the people in the area and the needs of the community. As only a teacher can, Ms. Rodriquez has experienced the struggles and needs of all diverse groups within the town. She has the knowledge and compassion to guide others ideas and desires. All her students have heard her say "You are what you put into it."
